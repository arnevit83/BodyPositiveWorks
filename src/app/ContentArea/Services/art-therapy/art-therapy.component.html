What is Art Therapy?


Art therapy doesn’t require artistic talent. It uses the power of the creative process as a vehicle for healing, communication, self-expression, celebration and personal development.

Its purpose is to help people better understand their feelings and behavior and resolve deeply held issues. It is a positive way to access, explore, express and heal inner feelings, stresses and experiences not available through other means and as such is a powerful way of knowing yourself.

Using creative techniques such as drawing, painting, collage, clay and coloring, our art therapist enables the student to express themselves artistically and explore the inner feelings and emotions feeding their art. The artwork can be used to help reawaken long buried memories and tell stories that may reveal unconscious beliefs.

Art Therapy Benefits


Art therapy has many benefits. It can help children, teens, and adults communicate and focus, relieve stress, anxiety and depression, unlock emotions, address trauma and PTSD, improve self-esteem, manage addictions, and cope with a variety of conditions from autism to mental illness.

Why Choose Us?


We offer group art therapy, and also a completely personalized service based around the individual’s needs. Our Art Therapist will get some background around why you have chosen art therapy and what she can offer, and together you will come up with a plan that works for you both.

Body Positive Works is a judgment free zone. Once you begin creating your art work, your Art Therapist will observe your process, without criticism. When you have finished, and sometimes while you are still working on it, your therapist will not put a value judgment on your work but will instead ask you to share your experience – how you’re feeling, what’s easy or challenging, and what’s coming up for you –unlocking feelings or emotions that might otherwise be hard to express.

Milissa Finkel, MS, MA, ATR-BC, LCAT
Art therapy is a specialized modality using the power of the creative process as a vehicle for healing, communication, self-expression, celebration and personal development. It is a positive way to access, explore, express and heal inner feelings, stresses and experiences not available through other means and, as such, is a powerful way of knowing yourself.

Milissa creates a safe, non-judgmental environment in which to build a supportive and caring relationship. A variety of materials are offered including paints, drawing, clay and collage to help access inner feelings and experiences. She uses both the art experience in conjunction with talking therapy to facilitate self-expression, change and growth. This unique approach helps people of all ages. No art experience is necessary, only an openness for self-reflection and transformation.

Let Milissa show you how this powerful process of creative expression can help you access, understand and change your life – helping you become happier and more fulfilled.

Milissa Finkel is a Board Certified, Licensed and Registered Art Therapist and NY Licensed Creative Arts Therapist. She holds a graduate degree in Expressive Arts Therapy with a specialization in Art Therapy from Lesley University in Cambridge MA. In addition, she holds a graduate degree in Early Childhood, Special Education. Milissa has worked for over 20 years providing individual art psychotherapy and group art therapy for children, adults, seniors and families in a variety of settings including private practice, schools, community centers, senior centers and hospitals.
