How homeopathy works

Homeopathy is based on the principle of Law of Similar or “Like cures like”- a concept that dates back to the Greek Father of Medicine, Hippocrates. The word is derived from the Greek word “homeo”, which means “same/similar”, and “pathos” which means suffering.

So, a homeopathic remedy is used to treat the symptoms that the remedy would actually cause in a healthy person. For example, in healthy individuals, cutting an onion often results in a runny nose and teary eyes. Therefore, the homeopath would use a homeopathic remedy made of onion (Allium Cepa) for an individual who is exhibiting these symptoms, perhaps caused by seasonal allergies or by respiratory discomfort. The homeopathic remedies we use have been sourced from nature and then diluted and potentized and, once a remedy reaches a certain dilution, the healing power of the remedies works on a much deeper level.

Our homeopathic remedies are prepared according to the U.S. FDA recognized Homeopathic Pharmacopoeia.
