What Is Yoga?



There is a common misunderstanding that yoga is all about fancy poses – standing on one hand or twisting your body into a pretzel. While this may have a place on Instagram, it doesn’t have a place in our studio because it’s not what yoga is to us.

To us yoga is a tool for modern life; a way for everyone - regardless of shape, size or age - to listen to, respect and honor their body. If you can breathe you can do yoga!

It’s also a tool to honor the mind – why let all that negative chatter get in the way of having a great life? Yoga helps us rise above all that chatter, so we can be still. With that stillness it's a lot easier (believe us) to see clearly, choose wisely, and act courageously – to handle any curve balls life might throw your way.

For us yoga is also about community. If you’d asked the ancient sages what yoga is they’d tell you the word means “to yoke” – what they were talking about is unity. It’s about unifying our body, breath and mind on the mat, and taking it off the mat to create a deep sense of connection with our fellow human beings, and with our best self.



Benefits of Yoga



There are thousands of clinical studies on yoga now and they have proven there to be around 250 health benefits of yoga. At the root of it all is yoga’s effect on the parasympathetic nervous system. Let’s call it the PNS for short. Basically, the PNS lowers heart rate and blood pressure, which helps us relax.

That’s why the benefits of yoga include relief of any stress related condition – from anxiety and depression to PTSD and eating disorders. Think of it this way… every time you roll out a yoga mat, your nervous system is breathing a huge sigh of relief.

Yoga’s benefits are endless; improved flexibility, balance, sleep, mood, memory, attitudes to food, energy levels, self-esteem, and even helping us cope better with a wide range of health conditions – from migraines to diabetes to dealing with cancer.



Why Choose Us?



We take our reputation as one of the best yoga studios in NJ seriously. For our yoga instructors this means being there for everyone, and every type of body. We are body positive warriors (who also love cake), empowering and celebrating your path to loving yourself. There’s no cookie cutter approach; to us it’s really simple – everyone has a different body, so everyone needs to approach asanas differently.

It’s all about connecting with, accepting and celebrating your body, your mind and your spirit. That’s why our yoga classes emphasize listening to your body’s “voice”. In our yoga studio there’s no universal “right” way to do a yoga pose, and no judgment. You can go into a restful pose at any point during yoga class if that’s what your body needs. We teach every yoga student to “stay on their own mats” and not compare themselves with others, or what they “used to” be able to do.

Our studio is mirror free!  Our yoga is accessible to EVERY BODY.

What to Expect in a Body Positive Yoga Class


A body positive yoga class is about creating a feeling of being at ease in your body.

Whether you’re faced with an eating or body image issue, addiction, anxiety and/or depression, this class provides the philosophy and the movement to help you find peace within yourself.

What to Expect in a Kundalini Yoga Class


A Kundalini yoga class incorporates movement, dynamic breathing techniques, meditation, and the chanting of mantras to build physical vitality and increase consciousness. 

Kundalini has been found to be highly effective for the physical and emotional journey of healing from addictions, eating disorders, anxiety and depression. 

What to Expect in a Yin Yoga Class


In this yoga class, the focus is on holding poses that target the connective tissues, bones, ligaments and joints…parts deep in our bodies that are not stretched or exercised in more active yoga classes. 

  As we hold issues in our tissues, this practice allows for the release of deeper physical and emotional blockages within the body. 

What to Expect in a Relax and Reset Class


During this gentle class, we release tension, stretch, breathe and reconnect with a feeling of peace and ease. 

This class allows our body and mind to take a pause, which is essential to the healing process.

What to Expect in a Yoga for Addiction Recovery Class


Yoga shows us how to sit with and work through discomfort or difficult emotions rather than numb ourselves with food, alcohol, drugs, shopping, or any other addiction or vice.

Join us for this unique yoga and meditation practice, accompanying you on your path to recovery. 

The theme of the class is the 12 step principles, addiction recovery and support in case of relapse.

Yin Yoga for Addiction Recovery Class


The long holds in Yin Yoga shows us how to sit with and work through discomfort or difficult emotions, releasing deeply held physical and emotional blockages within the body and supporting you on the path to recovery.

The theme of the class is the 12 step principles, addiction recovery and support in case of relapse.

What to Expect in a Yoga for Recovery from Eating Disorders, Disordered Eating & Negative Body Image Class


Yoga philosophy teaches us to view the body as a precious, yet temporary, home for our souls. 

In this class we explore how yoga can teach us to view our bodies in a more positive light.

The theme of the class always focuses on ways we can start to heal the relationship between mind and body.

Jen Kraft
Types of yoga classes taught:
Body Positive Yoga, Yoga for Recovery from Eating Disorders, Disordered Eating and Negative Body Image

With a BA in English from the University of Michigan, and an MBA in Marketing from the University of Pennsylvania’s Wharton School, Jen launched a successful career as a marketing professional in Manhattan. She began her yoga practice after her third knee surgery at the age of 39. Originally there for the physical benefits, she soon realized that the philosophy behind the practice was helping her make peace with the negative body image issues she had struggled with since her teen years.

Eager to share yoga’s benefits, Jen decided to train as a yoga teacher and received her 200-hour teacher certification from Ben Wisch at Flow Yoga in Ho-Ho-Kus, N.J., and opened her own studio, Defiant Yoga, with the mission of helping individuals of all ages learn to love and accept themselves exactly as they are. She is proud to expand this mission as co-founder of Body Positive Works, an integrative healing and wellness sanctuary located in Saddle River, NJ.

Jen has taken advanced trainings with Dr. Melody Moore of the Embody Love Movement, Leslie Kaminoff of Yoga Anatomy and Charlotte Stone of the Stone Center for Yoga and Health. She is currently working on her 500 hour certification with Jody Domerstad of Become One Wholeness and so far has completed Neurosculpting®, Yin Yoga Level One, and Functional Anatomy.

Jen was also instrumental in launching Kula for Karma’s Yoga for Eating Disorder Recovery program. She co-wrote the manual and teaches the Yoga for Recovery from Eating Disorders advanced teacher trainings. She is passionate about this work and continues to teach Kula’s Yoga for Eating Disorders weekly at Body Positive Works.

In her yoga classes, Jen’s main priority is to help students hear their bodies’ signals and adjust the practice to their unique needs in a welcoming, non-competitive space. Jen focuses on the purpose of the poses and how they feel rather than how they look. Students are encouraged to look at the language they use towards their bodies (there is no such thing as a “bad” hip!) and to “stay on their own mats” (don’t compare!). Jen knows from personal experience that while embracing one’s body can be a challenging, lifelong journey, a regular yoga practice reminds us that our bodies deserve to be treated with the utmost kindness, love and respect.
