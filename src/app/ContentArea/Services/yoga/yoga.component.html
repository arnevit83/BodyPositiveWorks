What Is Yoga?



There is a common misunderstanding that yoga is all about fancy poses – standing on one hand or twisting your body into a pretzel. While this may have a place on Instagram, it’s not what yoga is to us body positive warriors, here in Bergen County.

For us yoga is a tool for modern life; a way for everyone - regardless of shape, size or age - to listen to, respect and honor their body. If you can breathe you can do yoga!

It’s also a tool to honor the mind – why let all that negative chatter get in the way of having a great life? Yoga helps us rise above all that chatter, so we can be still. With that stillness it's a lot easier (believe us) to see clearly, choose wisely, and act courageously – to handle any curve balls life might throw your way.

For us yoga is also about community. If you’d asked the ancient sages what yoga is they’d tell you the word means “to yoke” – what they were talking about is unity. It’s about unifying our body, breath and mind on the mat, and taking it off the mat to create a deep sense of connection with our fellow human beings, and with our best self.



Benefits of Yoga



There are thousands of clinical studies on yoga now and they have proven there to be around 250 health benefits of yoga. At the root of it all is yoga’s effect on the parasympathetic nervous system. Let’s call it the PNS for short. Basically, the PNS lowers heart rate and blood pressure, which helps us relax.

That’s why the benefits of yoga include relief of any stress related condition – from anxiety and depression to PTSD and eating disorders. Think of it this way… every time you roll out a yoga mat, your nervous system is breathing a huge sigh of relief.

Yoga’s benefits are endless; improved flexibility, balance, sleep, mood, memory, attitudes to food, energy levels, self-esteem, and even helping us cope better with a wide range of health conditions – from migraines to diabetes to dealing with cancer.



Why Choose Us?



We take our reputation as one of the best yoga studios in NJ seriously. For us this means being there for everyone, and every type of body. We are body positive warriors (who also love cake), empowering and celebrating your path to loving yourself. There’s no cookie cutter approach; to us it’s really simple – everyone has a different body, so everyone needs to approach asanas differently.

It’s all about connecting with, accepting and celebrating your body, your mind and your spirit. That’s why our yoga classes emphasize listening to your body’s “voice”. In our yoga studio there’s no universal “right” way to do a yoga pose, and no judgment. You can go into a restful pose at any point during yoga class if that’s what your body needs. We teach every yoga student to “stay on their own mats” and not compare themselves with others, or what they “used to” be able to do.

Irene Maltzan Chac
Melanie, co-founder of Body Positive Works, is a psychotherapist whose practice is dedicated to working with adolescents, adults, and families who are dealing with eating disorders, body image, substance addiction, and stage of life issues. A Licensed Clinical Social Worker and Licensed Clinical Alcohol and Drug Counselor with 25 years of direct practice experience, she holds the highest New Jersey licensure in both disciplines.

Over the years, Melanie has worked with clients in a variety of settings: hospital, crisis center, private non-profit agency, and private practice. She has gained a broad perspective and deep understanding of the challenges her clients face when seeking specialized services from multiple resources. The Body Positive Works was born of the realization that having a variety of services under one roof – and providing a strong sense of community – would support clients in achieving greater overall health, recovery, and long-term growth.

Melanie holds a Masters of Social Work from Columbia University and a BA in Psychology and Law and Justice from The College of New Jersey.
