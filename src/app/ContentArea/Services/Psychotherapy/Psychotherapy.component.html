What is Psychotherapy?


Psychotherapy is a talking therapy. Our therapists support you on your journey towards recovery, long term growth, health and wellness, ultimately seeking to bring you home to yourself.

Whether you are dealing with anxiety or depression, an eating disorder or substance addiction, body image or adolescent issues, a divorce or the loss of a loved one, our expert psychotherapists can help.

Therapy sessions are always confidential, so you can talk to your therapist about things that might feel uncomfortable otherwise.

Choose whether to see our therapists on your own, with a family member, or to join a group for a sense of community and shared experience.

Benefits of Psychotherapy


Our psychotherapists work with you to help you express the way that you feel and gain insight into those feelings and the issues behind them. The benefits of therapy are to help you cope with the challenges you are facing, and to find new ways to think about those challenges - in order to improve your emotional and mental wellbeing.

Why Choose Us?


Our therapists have the highest licensure for psychotherapy in Bergen County NJ.

The Body Positive Works approach was born of the realization that having a variety of therapy counseling services under one roof – and providing a strong sense of community – would support clients in achieving better overall health, recovery, and long-term growth.

Holistic psychotherapy is our specialty - aiming to treat the whole person. Just as there is more than one approach to body work, there are many therapeutic approaches to the mind. There’s no one size fits all approach; no cookie cutters here. We use aspects of a multitude of modalities – including cognitive behavioral therapy, behavioral therapy, integrative therapy, holistic therapy, mindfulness-based cognitive therapy, and positive therapy, to treat the individual.

Chani Getter, MSW, LSW
Chani is a licensed social worker and an ordained Interfaith Minister who was raised in a Hassidic Jewish home.

Chani provides therapy for individuals, couples and families. She is especially skilled in working with individuals who were raised in insular and/or fundamental religious communities who are struggling with the residual imprint of early childhood dogma. She has worked extensively with ex-Ultra-Orthodox Jewish, ex-Mormon, and ex-Muslim, and Ex-Fundamental Christian (especially those home-schooled).

Chani created the Family Justice program at Footsteps, which trains lawyers and therapists on how best to provide support for individuals with children who are leaving insular communities.

Chani facilitated a monthly support group for LGBTQIA women in NYC for 13 years. She directed and facilitated weekend retreats for LGBTQIA individuals and families for over 7 years.

Chani works with individuals who have past trauma, be it religious, sexual abuse or other. She understands the impact that trauma has on the brain and body, and that it can affect every aspect of daily life even years afterwards. She helps clients to build a life beyond the limitations they’ve encountered.
